What's Included

The Most Comprehensive Health Assessment in Boston

01

Advanced Bloodwork (60+ Biomarkers)

Full metabolic panel, lipid panel with ApoB and Lp(a), inflammatory markers (hsCRP, homocysteine), hormonal panel (thyroid, testosterone, estrogen, cortisol, DHEA-S, IGF-1), metabolic markers (fasting insulin, HbA1c, glucose), nutrient status (vitamin D, B12, folate, ferritin, magnesium), kidney and liver function, CBC with differential.

Most annual physicals check fewer than 20 markers. We check 60+. This is how early-stage risks like insulin resistance, subclinical thyroid dysfunction, and elevated ApoB get caught years before symptoms appear.

Learn more about the bloodwork panel →
02

DEXA Body Composition Scan

Dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry measures total body fat percentage, regional fat distribution, lean muscle mass by limb, visceral adipose tissue (VAT), and bone mineral density.

Scale weight tells you almost nothing. DEXA gives you the complete picture. Visceral fat, lean mass distribution, bone density. Your Healthspan Specialist uses this data alongside your bloodwork to identify metabolic risks that would otherwise go undetected.

Learn more about DEXA scanning →
03

Clinical VO2 Max Test

Medical-grade metabolic cart with breathing mask, graded exercise protocol (treadmill or bike), breath-by-breath gas exchange analysis. Measures VO2 max (mL/kg/min), ventilatory thresholds (VT1 and VT2), respiratory exchange ratio, and metabolic substrate utilization.

VO2 max is the single strongest predictor of all-cause mortality. Wearable estimates can be off by 10-20%. A clinical test gives you ground-truth data and precise heart rate training zones based on YOUR physiology, not age-based formulas.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

What is the Longevity Audit?

The Longevity Audit is a comprehensive health assessment that combines advanced bloodwork (60+ biomarkers), a DEXA body composition scan, a clinical VO2 max test, and a Healthspan Specialist review into a single assessment. It gives you a complete baseline of your metabolic, cardiovascular, and physical health, along with a personalized action plan.

How long does the process take?

Assessments are completed in 1-2 visits over about a week. Results are reviewed with your Healthspan Specialist within 2 weeks of your final assessment.

Is this a membership?

No, it's a one-time assessment. Many patients choose to continue with Protocol's ongoing care after seeing their results, but there's no obligation.

How is this different from an annual physical?

Annual physicals check fewer than 20 markers. The Longevity Audit checks 60+, adds DEXA and VO2 max, and interprets everything through a longevity medicine lens. Your Healthspan Specialist connects the dots across all data points.

Where are the assessments done?

All testing is completed at partner facilities in the greater Boston area. Specific locations are confirmed after booking.
