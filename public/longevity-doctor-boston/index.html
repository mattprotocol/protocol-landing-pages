Why Longevity Medicine? Because Reactive Healthcare Is Not Enough.

Most healthcare operates on a simple premise: wait until something breaks, then fix it. Your annual physical is designed to catch disease after it has already developed. By the time your doctor flags a problem, you may have been accumulating risk for years.

Longevity medicine flips that model. Instead of waiting for symptoms, we measure what matters early, set optimal targets based on the latest research, and intervene before disease takes hold.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

What is longevity medicine?

Longevity medicine is a proactive, data-driven approach to healthcare focused on extending healthspan, the number of years you live in good health. Rather than waiting for disease to appear and treating symptoms, longevity medicine uses advanced diagnostics to identify risk factors early and intervene with evidence-based protocols before problems develop. It combines comprehensive biomarker testing, body composition analysis, cardiorespiratory fitness assessment, and ongoing monitoring to optimize your health trajectory.

What tests does Protocol use?

Protocol's baseline assessment includes over 60 biomarkers across multiple domains. Advanced bloodwork covers ApoB, Lp(a), full lipid subfractions, HbA1c, fasting insulin, metabolic panel, hormone panel, inflammation markers like hs-CRP, and key nutrient levels. We also perform DEXA body composition scanning for precise body fat percentage, lean mass, visceral fat, and bone mineral density. VO2 Max testing with metabolic cart provides a gold-standard measure of cardiorespiratory fitness. A functional movement screening identifies mobility and injury risks.

How is this different from anti-aging clinics?

Anti-aging clinics typically focus on cosmetic interventions, hormone replacement, and proprietary supplement protocols. Protocol is physician-led longevity medicine grounded in peer-reviewed research. Every recommendation is backed by published evidence. We do not sell proprietary supplements or make anti-aging claims. We measure specific biomarkers, set evidence-based targets, and track outcomes over time. The goal is disease prevention and healthspan extension, not turning back the clock on appearance.

Is longevity medicine covered by insurance?

Longevity medicine is typically not covered by insurance because it is proactive optimization, not diagnostic care for existing conditions. However, many HSA and FSA plans do cover the assessments and services included in Protocol's program. We provide documentation and superbills upon request. Many members find that investing in prevention is significantly less expensive than treating chronic disease after it develops.

How is Protocol different from my annual physical?

A standard annual physical typically involves a 7 to 15 minute visit, basic CBC and metabolic panel checking roughly 20 markers, and a conclusion of everything looks fine unless something is severely out of range. Protocol tests 60+ biomarkers using longevity-optimized thresholds, not just standard reference ranges. We add DEXA body composition and VO2 Max testing that your PCP does not order. You receive a personalized protocol with specific targets, and we retest quarterly to track your trajectory.

Who is this for?

Protocol serves health-conscious adults, typically ages 30 to 65, who want more from their healthcare than reactive sick care. Our members are often high-performing professionals who apply the same rigor to their health that they bring to their careers. If you are frustrated by annual physicals that tell you nothing, curious about your real biological age, or motivated to prevent disease before it starts, Protocol is built for you.

Where is Protocol located?

Protocol is based in Boston, Massachusetts. Diagnostic testing including DEXA and VO2 Max is completed at partner facilities in the Boston area. Your physician relationship includes both in-person and remote touchpoints.
