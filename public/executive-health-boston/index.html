What Is an Executive Health Assessment?

An executive health assessment in Boston is a comprehensive health screening designed for busy professionals who want more than a standard annual physical. It goes beyond basic bloodwork and a brief physician visit to include advanced diagnostics - the kind of testing that catches risks years before symptoms appear.

Most executive physical programs, including those offered by major hospital systems, were designed decades ago. They include a basic metabolic panel, a treadmill stress test, and standard screenings. They tell you whether you are “normal.” They do not tell you whether you are optimal.

Why Standard Executive Physicals Fall Short

A standard executive physical in Boston typically includes 15-20 biomarkers, a treadmill stress test that produces a binary pass/fail, and a BMI calculation. These tests were designed to detect existing disease - not to identify early-stage metabolic, cardiovascular, or body composition risks that can be addressed before they become clinical problems.

They do not test ApoB, the causal marker for atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease. They do not test Lp(a), a genetically determined cardiovascular risk factor that cannot be modified by lifestyle and is rarely ordered by primary care physicians. They do not measure VO2 max - the strongest predictor of all-cause mortality. And they interpret results against standard reference ranges, which define “normal” as “not actively sick” rather than “optimally healthy.”
