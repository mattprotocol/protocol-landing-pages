## What Is the Longevity Audit?

The Longevity Audit is Protocol's comprehensive one-time health assessment, combining advanced bloodwork, DEXA body composition scanning, and clinical VO2 max testing with a board-certified Healthspan Specialist review. Unlike a standard annual physical, the Longevity Audit tests 60+ biomarkers, evaluates them against longevity-medicine thresholds, and delivers a personalized action plan -- not just a "your labs are normal" sign-off.

### The Three Assessments

**Advanced Bloodwork (60+ Biomarkers)**

Most annual physicals check fewer than 20 markers using standard "normal" reference ranges. The Longevity Audit tests a full panel including ApoB, Lp(a), hs-CRP, homocysteine, HbA1c, fasting insulin, comprehensive hormone panels (thyroid, testosterone, estrogen, cortisol, DHEA-S, IGF-1), nutrient status markers, kidney and liver function, and CBC with differential. All results are evaluated against longevity thresholds -- not permissive population averages.

**DEXA Body Composition Scan**

Scale weight is one of the least useful health metrics. DEXA scans measure total body fat percentage, visceral adipose tissue (VAT), regional lean muscle mass by limb, and bone mineral density. Visceral fat is a primary driver of metabolic disease and cardiovascular risk -- and it cannot be detected by weight or BMI alone. Your Healthspan Specialist uses this data alongside bloodwork to build a complete metabolic picture.

**Clinical VO2 Max Test**

VO2 max is the single strongest predictor of all-cause mortality in published research. Wearable devices estimate VO2 max through indirect algorithms that can be off by 10-20%. A clinical test using a medical-grade metabolic cart and breath-by-breath gas exchange analysis gives you a precise measurement along with your ventilatory thresholds (VT1 and VT2) and personalized heart rate training zones based on your actual physiology.

### Who Is the Longevity Audit Right For?

- Professionals in Boston who want a comprehensive health baseline and don't trust a 20-marker annual physical to tell them where they actually stand
- Anyone with a family history of cardiovascular disease, metabolic disease, or early mortality who wants to know their risk profile now
- Athletes and health-conscious individuals who want ground-truth data on their fitness and body composition
- Anyone considering Protocol's ongoing concierge care who wants to start with a full picture before committing to a membership

### What Happens After the Audit?

Many Longevity Audit patients choose to continue with Protocol's concierge membership after reviewing their results. There is no obligation -- but the Audit is designed to give you a clear picture of where you stand and what to do about it. Your Healthspan Specialist will walk you through every finding and provide a prioritized action plan regardless of whether you continue with Protocol.
