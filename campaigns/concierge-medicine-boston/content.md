## What Is Concierge Medicine?

Concierge medicine is a membership-based approach to primary care that removes the constraints of the traditional healthcare system. Instead of rushing through a 7-minute appointment with a doctor who has 2,500 patients on their panel, you get a dedicated physician who limits their practice to give you the time, attention, and access you deserve.

In a concierge medical practice, your doctor typically maintains a panel of 200 to 600 patients — compared to the 2,000+ patients most traditional primary care physicians manage. The result: longer appointments, same-day availability, direct physician access, and care that is proactive rather than reactive.

### The Problem with Traditional Primary Care

The average primary care visit in the United States lasts 7 minutes. Your doctor has 15 to 20 patients to see that day, a waiting room that is always full, and a system that only pays them when something is wrong. Prevention, optimization, and the kind of deep diagnostic work that catches problems early — none of that fits into the model.

That is why 80% of chronic disease in America is preventable but rarely prevented. The system is not designed for prevention. It is designed for treatment after the fact.
